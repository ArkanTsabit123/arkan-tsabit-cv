The Business Challenge

Bejagoo Coffee was experiencing inconsistent monthly revenue and alarmingly low customer retention. Revenue would spike on weekends but drop significantly on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. The business had accumulated over two years of sales data in their POS system, but no one had ever analyzed it systematically. The owner relied on intuition and "what felt right" — which worked for survival but not for growth. My task was to change that.

Data Analysis Methodology

I started by extracting 12 months of transaction data from the POS system: 15,000+ transactions, product categories, timestamps, and customer IDs (from loyalty sign-ups). I used Excel and Google Sheets for analysis — no expensive BI tools needed.

Sales Pattern Analysis

I identified four critical patterns that became the foundation for our strategy:

  • Product Performance — Coffee dominated at 45%, but pastries had the highest margin (65% vs coffee's 55%).
  • Peak Operating Hours — 8-10 AM (breakfast crowd) and 7-9 PM (dinner) accounted for 60% of daily revenue.
  • Low Performance Periods — Tuesdays and Wednesdays were consistently 30-40% below average.
  • Customer Segmentation — Top 10% of customers (frequent visitors) contributed 40% of total revenue. This was a huge opportunity.

Customer Behavior Analysis

The most alarming discovery: 65% of customers visited only once per month. They'd come in, enjoy their coffee, and never return. Meanwhile, our competitors had 40-50% monthly retention. This represented a massive leakage in potential lifetime value. A customer who visits 4x/month is worth 4x more than a one-time visitor — and costs almost nothing to acquire once they're already in the door.

Additionally, I analyzed order patterns and found that 70% of customers bought only a single item. Very few added pastries or sandwiches to their coffee order — a classic upsell opportunity.

Data-Driven Solutions Implemented

1. Tiered Loyalty Program (The Game Changer)

Based on the segmentation analysis, I designed a loyalty program that rewarded frequency, not just spending:

  • Bronze (1-3 visits/month) — 5% discount after 5 purchases, free coffee after 10
  • Silver (4-7 visits/month) — 10% discount + free pastry every month
  • Gold (8+ visits/month) — 15% discount + free drink every week + birthday bonus

We promoted this via QR codes at the counter and social media. The psychology: customers now had a clear reason to choose Bejagoo over competitors, and a tangible goal to visit more often.

2. Menu Optimization

  • Discontinued — 3 low-performing items that sold fewer than 5 units per week (removed menu clutter).
  • Bundled meal deals — Based on purchase pattern analysis (e.g., "Coffee + Croissant = Save 15%").
  • Introduced 2 new items — Requested by frequent customers in a simple survey (Iced Matcha Latte and Avocado Toast). Both became top sellers.

3. Targeted Promotions for Low Periods

For Tuesdays and Wednesdays, we launched "Midweek Booster" — 10% off for loyalty members and double points. This alone increased Tuesday revenue by 28% within 4 weeks.

Measurable Results (6 Months After Implementation)

  • 35% increase in monthly revenue year-over-year (from ~$18,000 to ~$24,300/month average).
  • 40% improvement in customer retention — monthly repeat rate jumped from 35% to 49%.
  • 22% increase in average order value — from $6.20 to $7.55, driven by bundling and upsell.
  • Gold tier members (just 8% of customers) now contribute 28% of total revenue.
  • Customer satisfaction improved from 4.2 to 4.8 stars (Google Maps & GrabFood reviews).

Core Lesson: Data has no value without action. The most sophisticated dashboard is useless without execution. Start with a hypothesis, validate with simple analytics (Excel is enough!), then implement changes based on findings. You don't need big data or AI to drive 35% growth — you need curiosity and discipline.
