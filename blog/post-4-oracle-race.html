My Study Strategy and Daily Routine

I dedicated 4 to 6 hours daily for three consecutive months. Here's what my typical day looked like:

05:30 - 06:30 Morning review (flashcards + notes from previous day)
06:30 - 07:30 Hands-on lab on OCI Free Tier (build, break, fix)
19:00 - 21:00 Official documentation + video courses
21:00 - 22:00 Practice exams + community discussion (Discord/Reddit)

My structured approach included:

  • Hands-on Laboratory Work — Daily practice on OCI free tier. I rebuilt architectures from scratch multiple times until I could do it without documentation.
  • Official Documentation Review — Systematic reading of service guides, not just exam dumps. I focused on "Why" not just "What".
  • Practice Examinations — Completed more than 20 mock tests for professional-level certs. Reviewed every wrong answer deeply.
  • Community Engagement — Active participation in Discord study groups. Teaching others accelerated my own understanding.

Key Lessons Learned

  • Consistency Outperforms Intensity — One hour daily beats seven hours on weekends. Your brain needs daily reinforcement.
  • Hands-on Practice is Non-Negotiable — Theory alone won't pass professional exams. OCI Free Tier is your best friend.
  • Community Accelerates Learning — Discussing concepts reveals blind spots. Join Discord, Reddit (r/oraclecloud), LinkedIn groups.
  • Focus on Fundamentals First — Associate-level certs build foundation for professional. Don't skip them.
  • Sleep & Recovery Matter — Burnout is real. I took one full rest day per week and slept 7-8 hours nightly.

The "2-Pass Method" That Worked for Me

For each certification, I used a two-pass approach: First pass — watch all video courses at 1.5x speed to understand the landscape. Second pass — deep dive with official documentation + hands-on labs + practice exams. This method prevented me from getting stuck on minor details early on.

Resources Used (All Legitimate)

  • Oracle University — Official training courses (some free, some paid via my learning budget)
  • OCI Free Tier — Always-free services including Autonomous Database, Compute VMs, Object Storage
  • Official Oracle Certification Exam Guides — The blueprint PDFs are underrated — they tell you exactly what's tested
  • Oracle University YouTube Channel — Free deep dives and exam readiness sessions
  • Reddit r/oraclecloud — Community exam experiences and tips
  • Anki Flashcards — Spaced repetition for service limits, SLA numbers, and CLI commands
